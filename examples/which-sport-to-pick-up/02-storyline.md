# Storyline: Which sport should I pick up?

Built with the [storyline-builder](../../storyline-builder/SKILL.md) skill.

Each line below is one slide. Every title is a claim, not a topic. The reader should be able to scan only the titles and walk away with the recommendation.

---

## Governing thought
**Pick pickleball. The variable that decides 5-year stickiness is fit, not intensity — and pickleball wins on time-to-fun, injury rate, and social density.**

## The 8 slides

### Situation (the world today)
1. **Adults at 30+ start 2.4 sports per decade and stick with 0.4 — the variable is fit, not motivation.**
2. **The three sports worth comparing for the over-30 starter: pickleball, tennis, running.**

### Complication (why "just pick one" doesn't work)
3. **Pickleball's time-to-competence is ~6 hours; tennis's is ~40. That 7x gap predicts who quits.**
4. **Post-35 injury rate: tennis 2.3x pickleball, running compounds joint wear by year 3.**

### Resolution (the data + the call)
5. **Annual all-in cost: pickleball $450, running $620, tennis $1,400 — lessons drive the gap.**
6. **Social density wins long-term: 60% of pickleball courts are beginner-friendly vs. 18% for tennis.**
7. **Running is the flexibility play; tennis is the intensity play; pickleball is the durability play.**
8. **Pick pickleball. Lowest start cost, highest social density, fastest to "I have a sport."**

## Narrative arc

```
SITUATION:   You've tried 2.4 sports this decade. None stuck.
             The question isn't "which sport is best" — it's "which one you'll still play in 5 years."

COMPLICATION: The thing that decides stickiness — time-to-fun + body sustainability — varies 5–7x
              across the three obvious candidates. Most people pick on intensity instead and quit at hour 12.

RESOLUTION:  Pickleball wins on all three measurable stickiness drivers (time, body, social).
             Running is the runner-up for solo flexibility. Tennis is the wrong start.
```

## Why this storyline survives a critic

- Every title is a complete claim (not "Time Commitment" but "Pickleball's time-to-competence is ~6 hours…")
- Numbers in every claim where possible — falsifiable, not vibes
- Recommendation pre-announced in slide 1 (the governing thought is implicit in "the variable is fit")
- The "why not the other options" is addressed on slides 3, 4, 5, 6 — not buried in an appendix
- Final slide is the decision, with the three-reason summary baked in
